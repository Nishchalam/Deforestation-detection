# CNN History & Evolution Notes

This document traces the history of Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) from their biological inspirations to modern architectures.

## Key Milestones

| Year | Milestone / Model | Key Contribution |
|------|-------------------|------------------|
| 1959 | Hubel & Wiesel | Discovery of receptive fields in the cat's visual cortex (Simple vs. Complex cells). |
| 1980 | Neocognitron (Fukushima) | First neural network model utilizing shift-invariant local feature extraction. |
| 1998 | LeNet-5 (LeCun et al.) | First modern gradient-based CNN for digit recognition; introduced weight sharing and pooling. |
| 2012 | AlexNet (Krizhevsky et al.) | Scaled CNNs using GPUs, ReLU activations, and Dropout; started the deep learning boom. |
| 2014 | VGG16 (Simonyan & Zisserman) | Proved depth matters; standard 3x3 convolutions stacked together. |
| 2014 | GoogLeNet / Inception | Introduced parallel multi-scale convolutions (Inception module) and auxiliary classifiers. |
| 2015 | ResNet (He et al.) | Introduced residual (skip) connections, enabling networks with 100+ layers. |
| 2019 | EfficientNet (Tan & Le) | Discovered systematic compound scaling (width, depth, resolution). |
